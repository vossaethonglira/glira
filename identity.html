Identity
Identity & Authorization

A clear boundary map for recognition, access, and accountability.

The Identity system defines who may access which services, what claims may be made, and what responsibilities apply. Hover (or tap) the tabs in each row to reveal the operational meaning: definition, rights, and obligations.

Free Member
Entry-level participation (free)
Email-based account; may subscribe, join public activities, and access public standards downloads.
A registered user who participates in GLIRA’s public ecosystem without holding certification claims.
Access to public pages, transparency library, community updates, and open participation pathways.
Use brand language accurately, respect safeguarding principles, and follow platform conduct rules.
Candidate
In-assessment / evidence-building
Evidence portfolio required; claims are provisional until verified.
A participant undergoing SVOCF assessment or Study Tour role validation, with structured evidence submission.
Access to candidate guidance, portfolio templates, and assessment scheduling (where applicable).
Provide truthful evidence, protect third-party privacy, and accept audit/sampling rules.
Certified Holder
Verified credential holder
May claim credential within scope; subject to renewal rules and complaints/appeals procedures.
An individual whose competency claim has been assessed and verified under GLIRA standards.
Credential use within defined scope, listing (where applicable), and access to member-only tools.
Maintain integrity of claims, follow safeguarding/ethics duties, and cooperate with QA audits.
Affiliate Facilitator
Delivery role (non-assessor)
Facilitates learning activities; does not issue certification decisions.
A recognized facilitator operating under GLIRA guidance to deliver learning support and structured activities.
May be listed (if approved), use approved materials, and participate in delivery networks.
Follow safeguarding duty-of-care, use materials within license boundaries, and avoid misleading claims.
Assessor
Assessment & verification role
Applies rubrics and sampling rules; decisions are auditable and conflict-managed.
A qualified evaluator authorized to review evidence portfolios and produce verification outcomes.
Access to rubric tools, QA protocols, and case handling procedures; recognition within assessor registry (if used).
Declare conflicts of interest, protect confidentiality, ensure scoring integrity, and comply with audit checks.
Institutional Partner
Organisation-level cooperation
Partner onboarding and due diligence apply; brand listing is conditional and reviewable.
An organisation cooperating with GLIRA under institutional policies for delivery, accreditation, or recognition mapping.
Access to institutional templates, implementation plans, partner listing (if approved), and mapping addenda.
Implement safeguarding and data protection standards, accept QA/audit sampling, and handle complaints appropriately.
