Life Value Orientation
STA holds that the highest purpose of a study tour is not what learners have seen, but who they become and what responsibilities they are able to carry. Programme design should guide learners to make higher-quality choices in authentic contexts—choices whose impacts on self, others, society, and the environment are clearer and more accountable. Accreditation focus: clarity of value judgment, responsibility awareness, long-term perspective, and action consistency (alignment between words and actions).

Future Competence Readiness
Future competences is not an abstract slogan; it is a composite capacity to remain stable, learn, collaborate, and renew under uncertainty. STA emphasises that study tours must include uncertain tasks, real-world constraints, and reflective iteration, enabling learners to practise problem definition, solution generation, risk identification, and post-action review and improvement in complex settings. Accreditation focus: shock tolerance, transferable problem-solving, resilience and self-regulation, and maintaining structured action amid change.

Cross-Cultural Cognition & Learning
Cross-cultural competence is not simply “liking differences”; it is understanding how differences arise and how they shape judgment and collaboration. STA requires programme design to treat cultural differences as learning material: through observation → interpretation → dialogue → revision, misunderstandings are transformed into understanding, and conflicts into negotiation capability. Accreditation focus: boundary respect, de-stereotyping, contextual sensitivity, and culturally adaptive dialogue and collaboration

Cross-Disciplinary Learning Integration
Real-world challenges are inherently cross-disciplinary. STA requires study-tour curricula to elevate “knowledge points” into “system understanding”: using multi-disciplinary perspectives to build causal chains and structural maps, and then translating them into actionable practices and outcomes. Accreditation focus: systems thinking, evidence integration, extracting tools across domains to form workable solutions, and transferring learning into new contexts.
