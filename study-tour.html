Study Tour Accreditation

Accreditation for study tours and applied learning programmes

GLIRA accredits programmes based on clear standards: programme design, safeguarding & duty of care, risk management, evidence collection, and QA reporting. Accreditation is reviewed periodically.

Become an accredited provider Standards & downloads

Accreditation domains

Programme design

Learning outcomes, assessment plan, staffing, and learning evidence strategy.

Safeguarding-first

Duty of care, consent, age-appropriate practices, and incident escalation protocols.

Risk & QA cycle

Risk management, documentation, post-programme evaluation, and improvement reporting.

Typical accreditation cycle

1) Application & pre-review

Scope definition, document review, and verification planning.

2) Verification

Remote/onsite checks, interviews, and evidence sampling where appropriate.

3) Decision & registry

Accreditation decision with conditions, validity period, and reporting requirements.

4) Renewal & improvement

Periodic renewal with QA reporting and continuous improvement evidence.

GLIRA

GLIRA is a collaboration-focused certification and accreditation alliance. Membership supports standards orientation, evidence tooling, and cross-border coordination. Certification and accreditation are subject to verification and quality assurance.
