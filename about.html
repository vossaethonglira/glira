GLIRA

What we do

GLIRA is an international organization dedicated to advancing humanity’s competencies for sustainable development. Closely aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), we focus on the development of metacognition and metacompetence in individuals, organizations, and systems navigating an era of complex change—core competencies that include sensing change, self-regulation, adaptation, regeneration, and synergistic coexistence.

Grounded in the SVOCF (Symbiotic Value Orientation Competency Framework), GLIRA has established a meta-neutral, evidence-first global system for competency assessment and certification. This framework evaluates essential human competencies across five dimensions—physical, psychological, cognitive, social, and environmental—offering a trusted "common language of competence" for educators, certifying bodies, and social innovators worldwide. It does not presuppose any cultural or ideological stance but is anchored solely in verifiable chains of evidence.

Beyond assessment and certification, GLIRA actively supports and promotes cross-disciplinary innovation research related to these competencies, exploring the application of metacompetence in frontier areas such as artificial intelligence, educational transformation, organizational evolution, and community resilience, thereby infusing deeper momentum into the sustainable development of human society.

GLIRA has established partnerships with numerous universities, research institutions, and social organizations globally, working together to apply metacompetence frameworks in talent cultivation, organizational development, and social governance. We firmly believe that a sustainable future begins with the awakening and growth of humanity's inner competencies.
