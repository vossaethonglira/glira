Community Member
Definition: A member of the public who participates through community channels without registration or certification.

Benefits: access to public learning resources; join open online/offline events; subscribe to GLIRA updates; join interest groups for initial peer exchange; learn SVOCF pathways and requirements.

Registered Member
Definition: A registered GLIRA user with an account who can join projects and submit work.

Benefits: all Community Member benefits, plus member-only guides, toolkits, and templates; member discussion groups; submit reflections and receive formative feedback (Facilitator or AI-assisted); save a personal growth record; eligibility to apply as a Candidate; earn and redeem platform points (rules announced upon platform launch).

Candidate
Definition: An individual who formally submits an evidence pack and applies for SVOCF certification.

Benefits: all Registered Member benefits, plus an official Candidate guide and submission templates; coaching support from an assigned Facilitator (formative feedback); briefings on assessment process; formal assessment by an Assessor; certificate and digital badge upon passing; one free re-submission opportunity within a specified period; points can be used to offset certification fees and redeem learning resources.

Certified Holder
Definition: An individual who has passed SVOCF assessment and obtained a level certificate.

Benefits: all Registered Member benefits, plus a verifiable certificate (digital and paper options); optional listing in the public registry; permitted use of the title “SVOCF Level X Certified” under brand rules; access to holder-only community and advanced seminars; priority opportunity referrals from partners (when available); simplified renewal / CPD resources; eligibility to apply as Affiliate Facilitator or Assessor candidate; ongoing points accumulation (rules announced).

Affiliate Facilitator
Definition: A GLIRA-authorized individual who leads learning activities and supports evidence production in a defined program line (SVOCF / Study Tour).

Benefits: authorization letter and project toolkit; facilitator training and calibration; permitted use of “GLIRA Affiliate Facilitator” with program-line disclosure; access to de-identified learner data for coaching improvement; referral incentives (if applicable); co-creation feedback opportunities; pathway to Assessor or regional coordinator roles; points for delivery and calibration activities (rules announced).

Assessor
Definition: A strictly authorized professional who evaluates evidence against rubrics and issues certification decisions (restricted title).

Benefits: assessor credential and badge; advanced assessor training and calibration; permitted use of “GLIRA Assessor” with disclosure rules; assessment honorarium/fees by agreement; assessor-only case library and decision support tools; annual assessor summit participation; highlighted status in the public registry; points for assessment and calibration workload (rules announced).

Community Guide
Definition: A volunteer or part-time guide who supports onboarding, resource navigation, and learning guidance inside the GLIRA community.

Benefits: guide identifier; guide training and monthly exchanges; early access to new tools; ability to initiate themed activities with support; volunteer hours redeemable for resources or fee reductions; recommendation pathway to Facilitator candidacy; points for community contribution (rules announced).

Honorary Advisor
Definition: An honorary title for individuals with significant contribution or reputation; no operational authorization.

Benefits: honorary certificate; invitation to strategic sessions and annual celebration; public recognition with consent; non-binding consultation; priority access to publications; permitted non-commercial signature using the title under brand rules.

Institutional Partner
Definition: An organization (university, TVET institution, NGO, enterprise, etc.) with a formal cooperation relationship with GLIRA.

Benefits: cooperation memorandum defining scope; framework licensing for internal training/curricula; potential assessment site or training center after review; priority nomination for Facilitator/Assessor training; shared research insights; joint pilots and conferences; partner logo and profile display; possible governance participation by agreement.

Note: Titles and authorization claims must follow the Identity & Authorization boundary system. Points rules will be announced after platform launch.
