Partnership

Open collaboration, global partners, local nodes

The SVOC framework and programme accreditation mature through open cooperation. GLIRA partners support localisation, pilots, evidence quality, and recognition mapping. Local presence is organised through Liaison Nodes and Working Nodes.

Email partner proposal Governance & QA

Partner types

Recognition & mapping partner

Supports cross-border alignment, qualification framework mapping, and mutual recognition pathways.

Academic / research partner

Contributes evidence methodology, pilots, evaluation, and standards refinement through research.

Accredited provider

Delivers accredited programmes (e.g., study tours) with QA cycles, documentation, and safeguards.

Local nodes

Local Liaison Node

Best for organisations with public credibility and local networks: universities, associations, NGOs, and regional platforms. Focus: outreach, referral, briefings, and policy/local-context inputs (no credential issuance).

Local Working Node

Best for organisations with delivery and QA capacity: universities, training & evaluation teams, study tour operators with safeguarding. Focus: pilots, evidence collection, localisation of tools, and QA reporting (subject to audit and defined scope).

GLIRA

GLIRA is a collaboration-focused certification and accreditation alliance. Membership supports standards orientation, evidence tooling, and cross-border coordination. Certification and accreditation are subject to verification and quality assurance.
